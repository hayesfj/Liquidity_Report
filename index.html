Liquidity Analysis (40% weight) — Score 4.5/10

Net Liquidity = WALCL − TGA − RRP ≈ $6,725B − $880B − $8B ≈ $5,836B.

Every sub-signal leans bearish-to-neutral: the Federal Reserve Balance Sheet (WALCL) is essentially flat but still in slow QT (Chair Warsh publicly pushing for a smaller balance sheet, trimming long-end holdings). The Treasury General Account (WTREGEN) is rebuilding toward ~$880B, actively draining reserves. Reverse Repo (RRPONTSYD) is drained to ~$8B — the buffer that cushioned 2022–2026 QT is gone, so any further TGA build now hits reserves directly. Reserve Balances (WRESBAL) are falling (~$2.97T, −$259B y/y). This is the model's weakest pillar and the primary constraint on an upgrade. Not yet at reserve-scarcity stress, but the shock absorber is spent.

Financial Conditions Analysis (30% weight) — Score 4.5/10

Conditions tightened materially this week. Real yields (DFII10) sit near ~2.25–2.30%, in the "caution"-to-"restrictive" band (10Y nominal at 4.58%, highest since May, with oil-driven breakevens lifting nominals). DXY ~101.1 is in the restrictive zone (>101), strengthening on safe-haven flows and hawkish repricing. VIX ~18, up ~12% Wednesday but not stressed; MOVE elevated with rate volatility. The one genuine offset is credit: HY OAS at ~267–275 bps is historically tight with ~2.5% TTM defaults — benign. The tension: oil +~10% over two sessions has flipped rate-hike odds to ~70% for September and the FOMC minutes revealed a hawkish, divided committee. Real yields + a strong dollar are the restrictive drivers holding this score down.

Risk Appetite Analysis (30% weight) — Score 7.0/10

This is the model's strongest pillar and the reason the composite isn't Neutral. Credit risk appetite is firmly on — HYG stable, spreads tight, HYG/LQD near mean (no risk-off signal). Breadth is broad, not narrow: 57.3% of S&P names above the 50DMA (20DMA 62.8%), equal-weight (+12.2% YTD) beating cap-weight SPY (+10.5%). Small-cap confirmation is the standout — IWM +21% YTD, decisively leading SPY, historically a healthy-liquidity-breadth tell. New highs (5.0%) over new lows (0.9%) confirm contained downside. The only caveats: index-level concentration remains extreme (top 25 = 50.6% of SPY) and fresh-breakout participation (pocket pivots) is fading — broadening is real but not accelerating.

Current Regime: Selective Risk-On

Favored:

  • Small caps / SMID — the cleanest leadership signal (IWM +21% YTD); breadth and credit support them
  • Cyclicals & industrials — supported by broad participation, though vulnerable to the rate/oil shock
  • Quality growth — durable, but concentration risk is high and rate-sensitive at these real yields

Neutral / late-stage:

  • Speculative growth — allowed by tight credit but exposed if real yields push past 2.3%

Disfavored:

  • Long-duration / rate-sensitive assets (restrictive real yields, hawkish Fed)
  • Defensives (XLU/XLP) and cash — not yet the place to hide given intact breadth, but rising as hedges

Actionable Takeaways

What's working: Small-cap leadership (IWM), broad participation (equal-weight > cap-weight), tight/benign high-yield credit, contained new lows. The internals are genuinely healthy.

What's not working: The liquidity backdrop (spent RRP buffer, rising TGA, falling reserves, flat-to-shrinking Fed balance sheet); restrictive real yields (~2.25–2.30%) and a firm dollar (>101); an oil-driven inflation scare pushing September hike odds to ~70% against a hawkish Warsh-led Fed.

Conflicting signals (explicit): Risk-appetite internals (7/10) directly contradict financial-conditions/liquidity (4.5/10). Markets are pricing broadening and benign credit while rates markets price tightening — an unstable equilibrium. Credit (tight spreads) has not yet confirmed the equity-level caution, but Wednesday's VIX pop and spread-widening chatter are early cracks.

Upgrade to Full Risk-On requires: TGA drawdown or a Fed QT halt/pause that lets reserves rise (net-liquidity inflection); DFII10 back below ~2.0%; DXY below 98; and September hike odds collapsing. Confirmation would be HY OAS holding <250 bps with small-caps sustaining leadership.

Downgrade to Risk-Off triggers: HY OAS breaking above ~350 bps (the key mechanical line); IWM losing leadership and rolling under SPY; %>50DMA falling below 40%; DFII10 pushing above 2.4% with DXY >103; or a confirmed September hike. Credit is the tell — watch HY OAS first.
